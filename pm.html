particulate matter


alisa, brandon, and i were approached by the breathe project to design a series of materials to educate the general public about air pollution and particulate matter (PM).


pittsburgh has a long history of low air quality due to steel plants. while the city has come a long way since the 70s, there is still a lot of work to be done. when asked about air quality, many yinzers simply respond that it's been worse in the past. as a result, we chose to target an at risk group: children. by getting children interested and involved, their parents will be as well and motivated to change.


the first part was an educational video that visualizes a fairly abstract concept. the video is primarily informational and gives an overview of what PM is and its affects on the body.






the second part, the call to action, is a web based game that children can play on their own or with their parents. the game covers small steps that individuals can take to improve air quality. prototype is in progress.
