Wearable studies in biomimicry and proxemics

Clothing serves basic utilitarian purposes but also influeces our emotions and well being and can become a form of defense. Eileen and I designed and built interactive wearables that allow wearers to express stress and social discomfort through non verbal cues inspired puffer fish and squid.



A skirt increases the amount of space the wearer takes up, similar to a puffer fish's defense mechanism. A clear vest mimics cephalopod ink and conceals the wearer, and the bright pink is similar to the warning coloration of poisonous animals.
