Improving client efficiency and transparency

In the past year I've been working on improving internal efficiency and transparency with a portfolio of B2B clients. This has involved research, production, video and clickable prototypes, code proof of concepts, dashboards. One particular project was a interface refactor that resulted in an 80% decrease in data entry time.

Scope and Research

To kick off our partnership with the client, I met with leadership and staff, conducted observational studies and ridealongs, and studied internal training documents to understand their processes, relationships, and key business levers. By visualizing their business as a service blueprint, the client and I were able to discuss and identify process optimization opportunities.

In conversations with leadership, they listed sales person time usage as an area for improvement; cold calling clients vs data entry was eating up more time than they would have liked. Middle management was also stuck reviewing and revising entered data instead of focusing on strategy.(mention account gen)

early ideas included utilising OCR engine, but the complexity in the data ontology made it clear it was an excessively labourous task. problem wasnt that people were bad at interpreting but that the software interface didn't match the mental models of the invoice or the human. expressed challenged in training staff: they had to interpret documents and data as well as the software interface.
